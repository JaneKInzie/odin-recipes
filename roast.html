Crock Pot Roast with Potatoes and Carrots

This is a dinner you need to plan ahead for, but it is such a good feeling to come home from work to the smell of roast in the crockpot.

I put this going before work and let the crock pot do it's thing the whole day. Roast is a great meal for cold winter days...or to impress your mother-in-law. Tip: I line my slow cooker with a bag specially made for slow cookers, and it makes clean up so much easier.

Ingredients

  • 3 pound pot roast
  • 4 pounds red potatoes
  • One pound of baby carrots
  • Two large or three medium onions
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • One stick of butter
  • Beef boulion cubes
  • One pack powdered ranch dressing mix
  • One pack powdered brown gravy mix
  • Your favorite seasonings, such as garlic, rosemary, etc. (optional)

Steps

  1. Wash and cut potatoes into 1-2 inch chunks (I leave peal on, but you can remove if you prefer).
  2. Peal and slice onions. Add to bottom or slow cooker.
  3. Set roast on top of onions.
  4. Add carrots around roast. Put 4-5 beef boulion cubes on top of carrots.
  5. Add potatoes on top of carrots.
  6. Pour vinegar over roast.
  7. Pour ranch dressing mix and brown gravy mix on top of roast.
  8. Set stick of butter on top of roast. Do NOT add water.
  9. Slow cook on "high" for 7-8 hours.
  10. Remove roast and slice against the grain. Put in casserole dish with potatoes and carrots. Spoon drippings over top.
